Stay Safe Online  Checklist for College Students (India)

✔️ Create strong, unique passwords for each account (use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols; avoid using names or birthdays).
Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for your email, social media, and banking apps to add an extra layer of security.
Avoid clicking on suspicious links in emails, WhatsApp messages, or SMS, particularly those that create a sense of urgency (e.g., “Your account has been blocked.
Check the sender's identity before providing any personal or academic information, even if the message appears to be official.
Avoid using public Wi-Fi for important activities such as accessing bank accounts or conducting financial transactions.
Ensure your devices are up to date with the latest operating system, antivirus software, and applications to guard against current security risks.
✔️ Restrict the amount of personal information shared on social media to avoid potential misuse or identity theft.
Only download apps from reliable sources such as the Google Play Store or Apple App Store.

UPI / Financial Safety Tips:

💳 Never give out your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV to anyoneeven if they say they are from your bank.
Avoid approving unfamiliar "Collect Requests" on apps such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Keep in mind: getting money does NOT need you to enter your PIN.
Make sure to verify QR codes carefully before scanning them, particularly in stores or on online posts, to prevent being redirected to fake payment pages.

Report Cybercrime:

If you have been a victim of cybercrime, it is important to report the incident to the appropriate authorities. This helps in identifying and potentially preventing similar crimes from happening to others. Make sure to provide as much detail as possible, including any evidence you may have, to assist in the investigation.
